# 103 Search and Rescue Squadron

*[Voce originale su Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/103_Search_and_Rescue_Squadron)*

**103 Search and Rescue Squadron** of the [Royal Canadian Air Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Air_Force "Royal Canadian Air Force") is a [search and rescue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Search_and_rescue "Search and rescue") unit based at [9 Wing Gander](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CFB_Gander "CFB Gander") on the [Canadian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada "Canada") island of [Newfoundland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newfoundland_(island) "Newfoundland (island)"). The squadron is responsible for a large area covering the offshore waters of Canada's [Exclusive Economic Zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone "Exclusive Economic Zone") in the [Atlantic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean "Atlantic Ocean") and inland areas in the [Maritimes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritimes "Maritimes"), [Newfoundland and Labrador](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador "Newfoundland and Labrador"), and parts of [Eastern Quebec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quebec "Quebec"). The squadron operates three [AgustaWestland CH-149 Cormorant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AgustaWestland_CH-149_Cormorant "AgustaWestland CH-149 Cormorant") helicopters.

## History

The unit was stood up on April 1, 1947, at [RCAF Station Dartmouth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RCAF_Station_Dartmouth "RCAF Station Dartmouth") as the **103 Search and Rescue Flight**, a section of 101KU. Later that year, the unit moved to [RCAF Station Greenwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RCAF_Station_Greenwood "RCAF Station Greenwood"). In 1950, the unit was renamed **103 Rescue Unit** The unit was located in Greenwood NS until 1968 when it was moved to [RCAF Station Summerside](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RCAF_Station_Summerside "RCAF Station Summerside"), where it remained until unification of the [Canadian Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canadian_Forces "Canadian Forces") in 1968, when it was disbanded, with the remnants of the unit forming the new [413 Transport and Rescue Squadron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No._413_Squadron_RCAF "No. 413 Squadron RCAF") at [CFB Summerside](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CFB_Summerside "CFB Summerside"). As 103 RU it flew a variety of aircraft: [Canso-A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consolidated_PBY_Catalina#RCAF "Consolidated PBY Catalina") (until 1962), [Avro Lancaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avro_Lancaster "Avro Lancaster") (until 1965), [Douglas Dakota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douglas_C-47_Skytrain "Douglas C-47 Skytrain") (until 1968), [Noorduyn Norseman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noorduyn_Norseman "Noorduyn Norseman") (until 1957), [Sikorsky H-5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sikorsky_H-5 "Sikorsky H-5") (until 1965). 103 also had detachments in [Torbay, Newfoundland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torbay,_Newfoundland "Torbay, Newfoundland") (which was then [RCAF Station Torbay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._John's_International_Airport#Aerodrome "St. John's International Airport") re-opened in 1953 on the former RCAF Aerodrome – Torbay, Newfoundland) and [Goose Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CFB_Goose_Bay "CFB Goose Bay"), with the latter closed and becoming [107 Rescue Unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._John's_International_Airport#107_Rescue_Unit_RCAF "St. John's International Airport") in 1954. RCN provided high-speed launch vessels (ex-RCMP) support at select locations.

The unit was re-activated in 1977 at [CFB Gander](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CFB_Gander "CFB Gander") to meet the search and rescue demands in Canada's area of responsibility in the western [North Atlantic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Atlantic "North Atlantic"). At the time of its reactivation, the squadron was equipped with the [Boeing Vertol CH-113 Labrador](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boeing_Vertol_CH-113_Labrador "Boeing Vertol CH-113 Labrador") twin-rotor helicopter and was stationed in a permanent hangar constructed on the grounds of the airport in 1977. In 1997 the squadron was re-designated to its current name by [Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh "Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh"), and presented with its standard.

In 2009, the squadron, alongside its parent unit 9 Wing Gander, hosted [SAREX 09](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SAREX_(US_military)?action=edit&redlink=1 "SAREX (US military)"), an annual search and rescue exercise involving SAR organizations across Canada, with units from the [United States Air Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Air_Force "United States Air Force") and international observers also attending.
